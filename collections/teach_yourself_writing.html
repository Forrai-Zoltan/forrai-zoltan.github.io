Why Should you Learn Writing?

A person's inner world is quite remarkable. You have all these incredible ideas, feelings, and thoughts in there. They make up the person that you are. They are, by all means, quite cool.

The problem is that they are all locked away, accessible only by you. I will never know what's going on in there, just as you'll never know my inner world. We can never experience what it is like to be someone else, to have their thoughts and feelings, to see the world as they see it. This is the sad truth of our reality: you'll only ever know your own inner world, and your inner world will only ever be known by you.

Language, however, can act as a mediator between your mind and mine. By using your words, you can give a representation of your inner world in the form of language, and by speaking it to me, that mental image can be recreated in my head as well. Words are truly wondrous, powerful things. Through speaking, we can share ideas, feelings, and thoughts, recreating them in other people's minds. This is the closest we can get to each other's inner world: the spoken word.

But there is a flaw in speech. You can't always be there to walk someone through your thoughts. You would also get really tired of repeating yourself all the time. The weakness of speech is that it is bound by time and space.

This problem was recently solved by the invention of audio recording. But we didn’t always have this. An earlier solution was to represent speech with symbols, aka writing.

Writing is quite incredible. Not bound by time and space, I can translate my thoughts to you without needing to be around. I can be long dead, and parts of my inner world would still be accessible to you through my writing. Aristotle, Caesar, Shakespeare, Voltaire; we still have access to their minds because they wrote things down. This is quite something, isn’t it?

However, there is another flaw true for both speech and its symbolic representation. Because your inner world is so infinitely complex, mere words cannot do it justice. Speech can never represent your inner world one-to-one. It can only approximate. Some meaning will always be lost; that is just the nature of language.

Not all is lost, though. While we can never fully translate our thoughts through language, we can get close. We can learn how to use language, spoken or written, in ways that better represent our inner world. Through the right words — those with nuance, shades of meaning, poetry, and rhythm — we can give others glimpses into our inner worlds if we master language.
