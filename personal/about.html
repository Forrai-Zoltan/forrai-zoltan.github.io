About the Website

It was in the quiet wake of the great pestilence, when time was shackled and stood still, when people were imprisoned by an edict of solitude. Therein, I conceived of a place—a hermitage—built not by mortar nor stone but by words and coded cant. I made it be a library, a place to trace and revisit my notes on writing and wizardry. That humble abode grew into what you behold now.

I erected this edifice to liberate myself from three vices of the modern web:

  1. To evade the argus-eyed gaze of social media’s cyclopean surveillance.
  2. To elude the squalling squibs of lousy ads and the propagation of odious ideas.
  3. To escape the mesmeric pleasures that prey on your weakened volition.

What You can Expect

Verily, I find it somewhat difficult to define what this website may be. It’s not a book blog although I do review books. Not a writer’s portfolio, although I do self-publish my works on here. And certainly not a plain weblog either, although, again, I do share essays and update a micro-blog. Let’s simply call it a literary website.

However, as stated at the top of every page, I’m a full time writer not. My schedule is awash and drowning in responsibilities, and I cannot yet commit myself to lengthy literary labour. My lettered yield is yet sporadic. But, should you savour this style of writing, I can promise to continue with my relentlessly purple prose and perfumed poetry, and provide you with the weirdest of fiction, free of charge.
