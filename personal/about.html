About the Website

It was in the quiet wake of the great pestilence, when time was shackled and stood still, when people were imprisoned by an edict of solitude. Therein, I conceived of a place—a hermitage—built not by mortar nor stone but by words and coded cant. I made it be a library, a place to trace and revisit my notes on writing and wizardry. That humble abode grew into what you behold now.

I erected this edifice to liberate myself from three vices of the modern web:

  1. To evade the argus-eyed gaze of social media’s cyclopean surveillance.
  2. To elude the squalling squibs of lousy ads and the propagation of odious ideas.
  3. To escape the mesmeric pleasures that prey on your weakened volition.

What You can Expect

Verily, I find it somewhat difficult to define what this website may be. It’s not a book blog although I do review books. Not a writer’s portfolio, although I do self-publish my works on here. And certainly not a plain weblog either, although, again, I do share essays and update a micro-blog. Let’s simply call it a literary website.

However, as stated at the top of every page, I’m a full time writer not. My schedule is awash and drowning in responsibilities, and I cannot yet commit myself to lengthy literary labour. My lettered yield is yet sporadic. But, should you savour this style of writing, I can promise to continue with my relentlessly pretentious purple prose and perfumed poetry, and provide you with the weirdest of fiction, free of charge.

Who am I?

In part, this website exists to help me answer this question. To go over the basics: my real name is Zoli, but online I usually prefer to use my pseudonym, Gildrom.1 I’m European, born in Hungary, with a plan to expatriate to Ireland, hopefully soon. I’m in my mid-to-late twenties, pushing 27 as I’m writing this.

In high school, I studied media and filmmaking, but for various reasons, I was left unsure whether to continue with it. I spent one semester in film school at a reasonably good university, but after much disappointment, I left and pivoted careers.

My other love from those high school days was the English language. So naturally, I took to language teaching as a viable path forward. Now I study English and history at a more distinguished—albeit much more demanding—university. As I’m nearing the end of my studies, I’m happy with my choice, even if history is kicking my ass.

I think most people find me strange, but I don’t mind. I enjoy heavy metal and rock-adjacent music, anything from Alkaloid to Zappa. But I’m open to other genres too. I particularly like the music of Aurora, especially her singing voice. Still, my all-time favourite has to be Iron Maiden.

Although I’m not a huge gamer, I like mystery and narrative puzzle games—like Blue Prince, which is right up my alley. I’m a fan of older games like Morrowind, Thief, Legacy of Kain: Soul Reaver, and WoW Vanilla. I think my heart is stuck in the early noughties.

Regardless, my favourite pastime has to be reading. There is something about the simplicity and power of the written word that I find bewitching. I generally enjoy anything from mainstream pulp to Miltonic verse—though my choice of literature tends to be largely Anglocentric.
